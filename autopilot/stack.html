Keeping it running

Maintenance guide

Light upkeep — no code, nothing scary. The five things you might ever need to do.

1Check the agent is running›
Open OnlySocial and look at your scheduled posts. If you see posts lined up for the days ahead, the agent is working. The status strip at the top of this page also shows the last time it published. Or just text your Telegram bot "status" and it'll tell you. If posts have stopped, jump to step 3.
2Talk to it on Telegram›
Your agent has its own Telegram chat. Open it anytime to give instructions in plain language — make a post about Sunday's potluck, show me what's queued, approve all, or skip this week. It replies right back. This is the easiest way to steer the agent day to day without touching any dashboard.
3Restart it if it stops›
Open DigitalOcean and find your droplet. If it shows as off, click Power On — the agent picks back up on its own. If it was already on but posts stopped, the cause is almost always step 5 (an OpenAI billing limit), so check there next.
Don't delete or rebuild the droplet. Powering off and on is safe. Destroying it removes the agent.
4Move to a bigger server (only if you outgrow it)›
The $4 droplet handles a normal posting schedule easily. If you ramp way up across many channels, open the droplet in DigitalOcean → Resize → pick the next size up → confirm. Takes a couple minutes; nothing else changes. Most churches never need this.
5Swap or refill your OpenAI key›
Captions and images bill to your own OpenAI account, so they stop if it hits a spending limit. Open OpenAI → Usage to see your balance, or Billing to add funds. If you make a new key, send it to us and we'll drop it in — quick managed task, or we'll show you where it lives.
Heads up: OpenAI is the one cost that's truly yours and usage-based — normally just a few dollars a month. If posting suddenly stops, check here first.
