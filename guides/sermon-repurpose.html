Turn one sermon into ten pieces of content

Your pastor already did the hard work on Sunday. Let's turn that one message into a whole week of devotionals, posts, and study questions — in about 20 minutes, just by talking to AI.

Why this one's a game-changer

Every week, your pastor pours hours into a sermon. It's preached once on Sunday morning — and then, for most churches, it just… ends. All that wisdom, that study, that carefully chosen wording, gone after 30 minutes.

Here's the shift: that sermon isn't one piece of content. It's the source for a dozen. With AI, you can take that single message and spin it into devotionals, social posts, a blog article, small-group questions, and short video clips — so your congregation keeps walking with that message all week long, not just Sunday.

And the beautiful part: you're not creating from scratch. The thinking is already done. You're just asking AI to reshape it into different forms.

💡

This is "repurposing," and it's the pro move

Every big creator and ministry does this — one piece of content becomes ten. You're not padding or repeating; you're meeting people where they are, on the platforms and in the moments they're actually in.

1
Step one

Get your sermon into words

AI works from text, so first we need the sermon as written words (a "transcript"). Don't worry — you almost certainly already have what you need. Pick whichever you've got:

  • The pastor's notes or manuscript — the easiest. If it's already written down, you're ready.
  • A YouTube video of the sermon — YouTube auto-creates a transcript you can copy. iPro tipOn the YouTube video, click "...more" under the title, then "Show transcript." A text version appears on the right that you can select and copy.
  • An audio or video recording — free tools can turn speech into text. Even sending the recording to a tool like otter.ai or using your phone's transcription works.
💡

Don't overthink the transcript

It doesn't need to be perfect. A few typos or "ums" won't bother the AI at all — it understands the meaning. Just get the words in roughly one place.

2
Step two

Open your AI and paste the sermon in

Go to Claude or ChatGPT in a new tab. This time, instead of typing a short request, you're going to paste your whole sermon into the message box first, then add your instructions. The AI can happily read a long sermon — that's its specialty.

claude.ai
✦ Claude
"This Sunday we're looking at the parable of the prodigal son… [your whole sermon pasted here] …and that's the heart of the Father's love." ↑
👆 Paste the full sermon text right into the box. Long is fine — that's what it's built for.

Don't press Enter just yet — first we'll add what we want it to make. That's the next step.

3
Step three

Add this — your "ten pieces" prompt

Right after the sermon text you pasted, add the prompt below (copy it, paste it underneath, fill in the blue blanks). Then press Enter. iPro tipOrder matters a little: sermon first, then this instruction underneath. That way the AI reads the message, then knows what to do with it.

Repurpose prompt — paste below your sermon 
Above is the transcript of our Sunday sermon. I'd like you to repurpose it into a week of content for our church. Keep our voice warm and faithful to the message — don't add new theology, just reshape what's already here.

Our details:
- Church: [CHURCH NAME]
- Where we post: [Facebook / Instagram / both]
- Tone: [e.g. warm and encouraging]

Please create all of the following, clearly labeled:
1. A 1-paragraph summary of the sermon's main point
2. Three short daily devotionals (a few sentences each) drawn from the message
3. Five social media posts with captions and hashtags, each highlighting a different idea from the sermon
4. Five short, quotable lines from the sermon worth sharing as graphics
5. A short blog post (about 400 words) that expands the main idea
6. Five small-group discussion questions based on the message
7. Three ideas for short video clips we could pull from the sermon recording

Lay it all out clearly with headings so I can copy each piece easily.

Press Enter and watch it work. In under a minute you'll have a full content menu — devotionals, posts, quotes, a blog, questions, and clip ideas — all faithful to Sunday's message.

💡

It stays true to the sermon

Because you pasted the actual message and told it not to add new theology, everything it makes comes straight from what your pastor already preached. You're reshaping, not rewriting.

4
Step four

Shape any piece by talking

Got the whole batch? Now refine any part of it the same way you have before — just ask. The AI keeps the sermon in mind, so every change stays on-message.

  • "Write the blog post for people new to faith."
  • "Give me two weeks of devotionals instead of one."
  • "Add a kids' version of the main idea for our children's ministry."
5
Step five

Send each piece where it belongs

Now you've got a week of content from one sermon. Here's where each piece naturally goes — copy, paste, and you're done:

  • Social posts & quote graphics → schedule them in OnlySocial (that's exactly what Guide 02 covers — the two guides work beautifully together).
  • Devotionals → email them out through the week, or post one each morning.
  • Blog post → drop it on your church website.
  • Discussion questions → hand to your small-group leaders for the week.
  • Clip ideas → give them to whoever edits your video, with the timestamps to pull.
💡

Two guides, one workflow

Pair this with Social Media on Autopilot: repurpose the sermon here, then load the posts into OnlySocial to publish all week. One Sunday message, running on its own for seven days.

6
Step six

Make it your Monday routine

This works best as a simple weekly rhythm. Every Monday (or whenever suits you):

  • Save your repurpose prompt so you never rewrite it — just paste the new sermon above it each week.
  • Grab Sunday's transcript, paste it in, run the prompt.
  • Skim, tweak anything, and send each piece to its home.
  • That's your whole week of content — built on the message your church just heard.
💡

Twenty minutes, seven days of presence

One short Monday session keeps your church showing up online, in inboxes, and in small groups all week — all reinforcing Sunday. That's the kind of consistency that used to need a full-time staffer.

✦
Now get creative

One sermon can become even more

Once you're comfortable, push the idea further. The sermon is a goldmine — keep asking:

✦

A sermon series recap

At the end of a series, paste all the transcripts and ask for "a one-page summary of the whole series and its big takeaways." Great for newcomers catching up.

✦

A study guide from the sermon

Combine this with Guide 01: "Turn this sermon into a designed small-group study guide PDF." Now your groups study exactly what was preached.

✦

Captions for the clips

"Write short, scroll-stopping captions for each video clip idea above." Ready for Reels, Shorts, or TikTok.

✦

A follow-up for guests

"Write a warm follow-up email for first-time visitors that ties back to Sunday's message." Connection that picks up right where the sermon left off.

It's always the same rhythm you've now practiced three times: give it the source, ask for what you need, shape it by talking.

You did it. 🎉

You just turned one sermon into a whole week of content — and you know how to do it every Monday from here on. Sunday's message no longer ends on Sunday. That's a gift to your whole congregation.
