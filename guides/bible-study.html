Create a beautiful study guide with AI

First — what even is this?

You've probably heard people talk about "AI" or "ChatGPT." Here's all you need to know to begin: these are tools you talk to like a person. You type what you want in plain English, and they help you make it. No coding. No special words. If you can text a friend, you can do this.

For church materials, two of the best are Claude and ChatGPT. Either works great for everything on this page — pick whichever you like.

Recommended

Claude

Made by Anthropic. Wonderful at warm, thoughtful, well-organized writing — and great at formatting it beautifully. Find it at claude.ai.

Also great

ChatGPT

Made by OpenAI. Hugely popular and very capable. Works the same way for everything we'll do here. Find it at chatgpt.com.

Both have a free version that's plenty to start. Open one in a new browser tab, make a free account, and keep it open beside this page so you can follow along.

What are these actually for?

Think of Claude or ChatGPT as an endlessly patient assistant who has read more books than anyone alive and never gets tired of your questions. You can ask it to write, explain, summarize, brainstorm, translate, or design something — in plain words, like you'd ask a person. For a church, that means it can help you draft a study guide, outline a sermon, write a newsletter, plan a lesson, or design a flyer. Today we're using it for one thing: turning a passage into a beautiful, printable study guide.

💡

The one mindset shift that matters

You're not "operating software." You're having a conversation. Talk to it the way you'd ask a knowledgeable assistant for a favor — and when something's not quite right, you just say so. We'll practice that.

1
Step one

Gather your handful of details

Before we open the AI, jot these down on a sticky note. Having them ready makes the whole thing take two minutes. None of it is complicated — it's just the stuff you already know about your study.

  • The passage — book, chapter, and verses (for example, James 1:1–18). iPro tipNot sure which passage? Ask the AI for help: "Suggest a good passage for a study on patience." It'll give you options to choose from.
  • Who it's for — adult small group, youth group, new believers, a women's study, etc.
  • Your church name — exactly how you'd want it printed.
  • Your brand colors — if you know them. Don't worry if you don't; you can just say "navy blue and a bright blue accent" or skip it.
  • A tone — warm and conversational? Reverent and deep? Simple for beginners? Your call.
💡

Don't have brand colors?

Totally fine. The AI will choose a tasteful, churchy palette on its own. You can always say "make it more blue" or "use warmer colors" afterward.

2
Step two

Open the AI and start a new chat

Go to your AI in the other tab. You'll see a big empty box at the bottom of the screen with text like "Message Claude…" or "Ask anything." That box is where everything happens. You type there, press Enter, and the AI replies right above it — just like a text message thread. Here's roughly what you're looking at:

ChatGPT looks a little different, but the idea is identical — one message box, waiting for you.

If it's a brand-new conversation, even better — a fresh chat keeps things focused. Now let's give it our request.

💡

Don't worry if your screen looks different

These tools update their look often, and yours might be slightly different colors or layout. The one thing that's always true: there's a single box where you type your message. Find that, and you're set.

3
Step three

Paste this — your master prompt

This is the heart of it. Copy the text below, paste it into the message box, and fill in the blue blanks with your details from Step 1. Then press Enter. It asks for a richer, 3–5 page guide with proper page breaks, so it prints cleanly without any fiddling. iPro tipThe square brackets [ ] are just placeholders — delete them along with the text inside and type your own. Leave everything else exactly as it is.

Master prompt — copy & fill the blanks 
I want you to act as a thoughtful Bible study designer for my church. I'm going to ask you to create a complete, beautifully designed study guide that I can download as a PDF.

Here are my details:
- Passage: [YOUR PASSAGE, e.g. James 1:1–18]
- This study is for: [YOUR GROUP, e.g. our adult small group]
- Church name: [YOUR CHURCH NAME]
- Brand colors: [YOUR COLORS, or "you choose something tasteful"]
- Tone: [e.g. warm and conversational]

Please create a comprehensive, multi-page study guide as a single self-contained HTML page that I can save and print as a PDF. It should look professionally designed — clean layout, nice fonts, my brand colors, my church name in a header, and generous white space. Format it for 3–5 pages on letter-size paper (8.5 x 11 inches), and put a clean page break between each major section so it prints neatly with no awkward splits.

Include these sections, spread across the pages:
1. A title page — the study title, passage reference, my church name, and a warm 2–3 sentence welcome
2. Background & context — historical and cultural notes that help the passage come alive
3. The key verses, set apart and easy to read
4. Discussion questions — 7 to 8 questions that move from "what does it say" to "what does it mean" to "how does it change us"
5. A daily reflection section — a short reflection prompt for each of 3–5 days, each tied to a verse in the passage
6. A memory verse, with a little space to write it out
7. A closing prayer prompt, with room for notes

Make it spacious, warm, and genuinely ready to print and hand out. When you're done, briefly tell me how to save it as a PDF.
💡

Why ask for "a single HTML page"?

That's the magic words that make the AI build a real, designed document instead of plain text — and HTML is the easiest thing to turn into a clean PDF. You don't need to know what HTML is. You're just asking for it by name.

Once it's pasted and filled in, your message box will look something like this — then you click the send button (or just press Enter):

Now to get your PDF, the simplest path works every time:

  • Look for a Preview or Download option on the document it created, and open the preview so you can see the designed page.
  • With the preview open, print the page (on a Mac press Command + P, on Windows press Ctrl + P).
  • In the print window, where it asks for a printer, choose "Save as PDF" instead. Click Save. Done — that's your finished, designed guide.
👆
The trick most people miss: in the print window, change the "printer" dropdown to Save as PDF. That's how a web page becomes a polished file.

Because we asked for proper page breaks, your guide should land on 3–5 clean letter-size pages — no awkward splits, no margin tinkering. If the AI's reply included its own download button, you can use that too. Either way, you now have a real multi-page PDF you could hand out this Sunday.

5
Step five · the important part

Don't like something? Just say so.

This is the skill that changes everything. The first version is almost never the final version — and that's by design. You don't start over. You just talk back, the same way you're reading this. The AI remembers what it made and adjusts it.

Here's what a real back-and-forth looks like:

You
You
This looks great! Can you make the header a deeper navy blue and add a little more space between the questions?
AI
The AI
Done! I've deepened the header color and given the discussion questions more breathing room. Here's the updated guide.
You
You
Perfect. Could you add two more questions, and make the language simpler so it works for our high schoolers?
AI
The AI
Of course — I added two more questions and simplified the wording throughout for a younger group. Take a look.

That's the whole secret. You're the director; the AI is your tireless helper. A few things you can always ask for:

  • "Make it shorter / longer."
  • "Add our church logo at the top." (then drag your logo image into the chat)
  • "Make the tone warmer" or "more reverent."
  • "Add a section on how this applies to family life."
  • "Change the colors to match our website."
💡

You can't break it

There's no wrong thing to type. If a change isn't what you wanted, just say "actually, go back to how it was." Experiment freely — that's how you get comfortable.

6
Step six

Save the prompt — reuse it forever

Here's how this becomes a weekly five-minute habit instead of a one-time project. Copy your master prompt (the one from Step 3, with your church details already filled in) and paste it somewhere you'll find it again — a note on your phone, a Google Doc, a sticky note in your desk drawer.

Next week, the only thing you change is the passage. Everything else — your church name, colors, tone — stays the same. Paste, swap the verses, press Enter. A brand-new designed guide in under a minute.

💡

Build a little library

Save a few versions: one for adult groups, one for youth, one for a women's study. Each is just your master prompt with the "this study is for" line changed.

✦
Now get creative

The same skill makes a hundred other things

You just learned something bigger than study guides. You learned how to ask AI to write and design in one go, then refine it by talking. That exact skill works for almost any church material. Try these next:

✦

A daily devotional

Same prompt, swap "study guide" for "a 5-day devotional on this passage, one short reading per day." Beautiful for emailing your congregation through the week.

✦

A "Who's Who" profile

Ask for "a one-page designed profile of a person in the Bible — their story, key verses, and what we learn from them." Great for newsletters or a youth wall.

✦

A sermon discussion sheet

Paste in your sermon notes and ask for "a designed one-page discussion sheet small groups can use to talk through Sunday's message."

✦

An event flyer or bulletin insert

"Design a one-page flyer for our upcoming [event], with these details…" — then refine the colors and wording by chatting, just like you practiced.

Every one of these uses the exact rhythm you just learned: tell it what you want, ask for it as a designed page, then talk back until it's right.

You just made a real, designed study guide using AI — and more importantly, you learned how to do it again with anything. That's a skill that'll serve your ministry for years.
