The big picture — what we're building

Keeping up with social media is one of the most draining jobs in a church. You know you should post regularly — but who has time to think up something every single day? Here's the good news: you can hand almost all of it to two helpers working together.

Think of it as an assembly line with two stations:

  • The writer (AI) — Claude or ChatGPT dreams up a whole month of posts in one sitting: encouragements, verse graphics, event reminders, questions to spark conversation.
  • The publisher (OnlySocial) — a scheduling tool that takes those posts and puts them out on Facebook, Instagram, and more — at the right times, on the right days, without you lifting a finger.

You set this up once. After that, it's about 30 minutes a month: generate the posts, drop them in, and your church's social media runs itself.

💡

You're the editor, not the machine

AI does the heavy lifting, but you stay in charge. You'll always glance over the posts before they go out — approving, tweaking, or swapping anything that doesn't sound like your church. Nothing posts that you haven't okayed.

①
Meet your tools

What's OnlySocial — and why use it?

You already met Claude and ChatGPT in the first guide — they're your AI writers. The new tool here is OnlySocial. It's a "social media scheduler," which just means: a place where you load up posts ahead of time and it publishes them to all your channels automatically, on a calendar you set.

Instead of logging into Facebook, then Instagram, then wherever else — every single day — you do it all in one place, once. And here's the part that makes it magic for us: OnlySocial can connect directly to AI, so the writing and the scheduling happen almost hand-in-hand.

Your writer

Claude or ChatGPT

Generates a month of posts at once — captions, ideas, hashtags, even tailored to your church's voice. Find them at claude.ai or chatgpt.com.

Your publisher

OnlySocial

Holds your posts and publishes them to Facebook, Instagram, and more on a schedule — automatically. Find it at onlysocial.io.

Take it one piece at a time

If automation sounds intimidating, relax — we'll set up the AI writing first and make sure that feels easy. The OnlySocial connection comes after, one small step at a time. You won't get lost.

1
Step one

Jot down a few details about your church

Just like last time, having a few things ready makes this fast. Scribble these on a sticky note:

  • Your church name and a one-line description (e.g. "a friendly non-denominational church in Pittsburgh"). iPro tipNot sure how to describe your church? Ask the AI: "Help me write a one-line description of my church for social media."
  • Your vibe — warm and encouraging? Bold and energetic? Calm and reflective?
  • What's coming up — any events, sermon series, or themes in the next month.
  • Where you post — Facebook, Instagram, both?
  • How often — a realistic number, like 3–4 times a week.
💡

Start smaller than you think

Three good posts a week beats seven rushed ones. You can always scale up once the rhythm feels easy.

2
Step two

Open your AI and start a fresh chat

Head to Claude or ChatGPT in a new browser tab. As before, you're looking for the single message box where you type. That's your starting line.

3
Step three

Paste this — your "month of posts" prompt

Copy the prompt below, paste it into the message box, and fill in the blue blanks from your sticky note. Then press Enter. iPro tipThe brackets [ ] are just placeholders — delete them and the text inside, then type your own. Leave the rest as-is.

Content prompt — copy & fill the blanks 
You're the social media coordinator for my church. I'd like you to plan a full month of social media posts.

About us:
- Church: [CHURCH NAME + one-line description]
- Our vibe: [e.g. warm and encouraging]
- Coming up this month: [events, sermon series, or themes]
- We post on: [Facebook / Instagram / both]
- How often: [e.g. 3 times a week]

Please create a month-long content calendar. For each post, give me:
1. The day it should go out
2. The full caption, written in our voice and ready to publish
3. A few relevant hashtags
4. A simple note on what image or photo would pair well

Mix up the types of posts: encouragements, a verse of the week, event reminders, a question that invites people to comment, and a behind-the-scenes or community moment. Keep the language warm and easy to read, and lay it out clearly so I can copy each post easily.

In a few seconds you'll have a complete calendar — every caption written, organized by day. Read through and enjoy the moment: that's a month of social media, done.

💡

Make it sound like you

If a post feels too generic, just say so: "Make these warmer and more personal," or "Add a couple that mention our youth group." Keep chatting until it sounds like your church.

4

  • "Make these shorter" or "give me longer, more reflective ones."
  • "Add emojis" — or "tone down the emojis."
  • "Write a week's worth about [your sermon series]."
  • "Give me 3 versions of this one so I can pick my favorite."
5
Step five

Set up OnlySocial (your publisher)

Now we hand those posts to the tool that puts them out for you. Go to onlysocial.io and create an account. Once you're in, you'll do two quick one-time things:

  • Connect your channels — link your church's Facebook page and/or Instagram. OnlySocial walks you through it with a "Connect" button for each.
  • Find the calendar — this is where your scheduled posts will live, laid out by day.

6
Step six · the automation

Load your posts and let it run

Here's where the two halves come together. You've got a month of posts from the AI, and OnlySocial ready to publish. Two ways to bring them in — start with the simple one:

The simple way (copy & paste)

  • In OnlySocial, click to create a new post.
  • Copy a caption from your AI calendar and paste it in.
  • Add a photo, pick the date and time, and choose Schedule (not "post now").
  • Repeat for each post. Twenty minutes later, your whole month is queued.

The advanced way (connect AI directly)

Once you're comfortable, OnlySocial has its own AI feature built in — so it can help write and fill posts right inside the calendar, cutting out the copy-paste. Look in its menu for an "AI" or "AI Assistant" area and explore. iPro tipSave the advanced setup for once the basics feel easy. There's zero rush — the copy-and-paste method works perfectly well forever if you prefer it.

💡

The best times to post

Not sure when to schedule? Ask your AI: "What are the best days and times to post for a church on Facebook and Instagram?" Then set your schedule around its answer.

7
Step seven

Make it a 30-minute monthly habit

You've done the hard part — the setup. Now it's a rhythm. Once a month, block 30 minutes and:

  • Save your content prompt (from Step 3, filled in) somewhere handy. Next month, just update "what's coming up" and run it again.
  • Generate the new month of posts, skim and tweak.
  • Load them into OnlySocial and schedule.
  • Done — your church shows up consistently online all month, while you focus on ministry.
💡

Batch it with your team

Make it a monthly coffee-and-content session with a volunteer. One person runs the AI, the other gathers photos. Thirty minutes, whole month handled.

Now get creative

The same skill unlocks so much more

You just learned to make AI write in your church's voice and to automate the busywork. That opens a lot of doors. Try these next:

✦

Turn one post into ten

Paste in your Sunday sermon and ask: "Pull 10 short, shareable quotes from this message for social media." Instant week of content.

✦

Reply faster

Ask the AI to "write 5 warm, on-brand replies I can use when people comment on our posts." Keep them handy for quick, friendly responses.

✦

A monthly email too

"Turn this month's posts into a friendly church newsletter email." One calendar, two channels covered.

✦

Graphics from words

Ask the AI to "design a simple verse-of-the-week graphic as an image I can post." A visual to go with your captions, no designer needed.

Every one of these is the same rhythm you just learned: tell it what you want, shape it by talking, then let your tools carry it out.

You did it. 🎉

Your church's social media is now on autopilot — AI writing the posts, OnlySocial publishing them, and you in the editor's chair. That's hours back every month, for good.
